Overconfidence in Frontier LLM Confidence Interval Estimates

Daniel Walters · Tulane University · 2026

A four-study empirical investigation of whether frontier large language models (Claude, GPT-4o, Gemini) produce well-calibrated confidence intervals when making real-world predictions. Studies 1–3 are completed cross-sectional analyses; Study 4 is an ongoing longitudinal live-tracking system.

Key Findings (Studies 1–3)

  • GPT-4o is the most overconfident model in every study (ECE: 43.7%, 40.4%, 18.9%)
  • Claude is best calibrated overall; Gemini is intermediate
  • All models show domain-confidence inversion: overconfident in equities and commodities, underconfident in crypto and forex
  • Claude and GPT-4o degrade with prediction horizon; Gemini holds calibration to 22 days
