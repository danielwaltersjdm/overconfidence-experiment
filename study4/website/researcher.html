About

Daniel Walters studies how people form judgments under uncertainty — particularly how overconfidence, memory biases, and missing information shape consumer and investor decisions. His research applies incentive-compatible experiments, field studies, theoretical modeling, and large-scale data analysis to questions at the intersection of behavioral economics and marketing.

He joined Tulane’s Freeman School from INSEAD, where he served as Assistant Professor of Marketing. He holds a PhD and MBA from UCLA’s Anderson School of Management and a BS from NYU’s Stern School of Business.

About this project

The AI Calibration Index grew out of a straightforward question: when frontier AI models express uncertainty through confidence intervals, are those intervals any good? The answer turns out to depend heavily on the model, the prediction domain, and the time horizon — patterns that echo the overconfidence literature in human judgment and decision-making.
