Confidence Across Domains

Does calibration depend on what you’re predicting? Three frontier AI models forecasted outcomes across five domains: stocks, crypto, forex, weather, and commodities. We scored 204 resolved predictions and found a striking pattern — models are overconfident in equities and commodities, but underconfident in crypto and forex.

What is the domain-confidence inversion?

All three models show overconfidence in familiar, data-rich domains like equities and commodities — their 90% intervals contain the true outcome far less than 90% of the time. But in volatile domains like crypto and forex, the pattern flips: intervals are too wide, producing underconfidence.

This inversion suggests that model uncertainty expressions are not well-adapted to domain-specific volatility. Models may anchor on equity-like uncertainty ranges regardless of the underlying domain.

μ < 1 = overconfident (intervals too narrow). μ = 1 = perfectly calibrated. μ > 1 = underconfident (intervals too wide).
