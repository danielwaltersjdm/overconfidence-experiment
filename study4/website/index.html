What does this mean?

When an AI model gives a 90% confidence interval, it is claiming: "I'm 90% sure the true value falls between these two numbers." If it really were 90% sure, then across many predictions, the true outcome should land inside the interval about 9 times out of 10.

The LLM CI Index measures exactly this. A hit rate near 90% means well-calibrated confidence. A hit rate well below 90% means the model is overconfident — its stated certainty exceeds its actual accuracy.

  • Target line (90%) — perfect calibration
  • Above 85% — well calibrated
  • 70–85% — slightly overconfident
  • Below 70% — substantially overconfident
