What do these metrics mean?

μ (mu, meta-knowledge ratio) measures whether a model's confidence intervals match the actual spread of outcomes. A μ of 1.0 means the model's stated uncertainty matches reality perfectly. Below 1.0 means overconfident (intervals too narrow); above 1.0 means underconfident (intervals too wide). Based on Soll & Klayman (2004).

Accuracy (normalized MAD) is the average prediction error as a percentage of price. Lower is better.

90% CI hit rate measures how often the true outcome falls inside the model's stated 90% confidence interval. Perfect calibration = 90%.

μ target = 1.0 — perfect calibration
