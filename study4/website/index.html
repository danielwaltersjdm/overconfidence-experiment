How this tracker works

Every trading day, three AI models (Claude, GPT-4o, Gemini) are prompted to predict closing prices for 200 US equities at three horizons: 1 day, 1 week, and 1 month. Each prediction includes a point estimate and a 90% confidence interval. When outcomes are realized, predictions are scored using the Soll & Klayman (2004) calibration framework.

The charts above show a 30-day rolling window: at each date, we compute metrics over the most recent 30 calendar days of scored predictions. This reveals how calibration evolves as models accumulate more prediction history.

μ < 1 = overconfident (intervals too narrow). μ = 1 = perfectly calibrated. μ > 1 = underconfident (intervals too wide).
