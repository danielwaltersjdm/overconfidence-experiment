What does this mean?

When an AI model gives a 90% confidence interval, it claims: “I’m 90% sure the true value falls between these two numbers.” If it really were 90% sure, the true outcome should land inside that interval about 9 times out of 10.

The LLM Confidence Index measures exactly this. A hit rate near 90% means well-calibrated confidence. A hit rate well below 90% means the model is overconfident — its stated certainty exceeds its actual accuracy.

Target line (90%) — perfect calibration
Above 85% — well calibrated
70–85% — slightly overconfident
Below 70% — substantially overconfident
